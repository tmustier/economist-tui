The Bank return showed increases of £585,000 in the stock of gold, £286,000 in the note circulation, and £299,000 in the reserve. The total securities rose by £3,312,000, and deposits were £3,629,000 higher, Other rising by £4m while there was a small decline in the Public. The currency notes account showed notes and certificates outstanding rising by £1,369,758 to £295,160,730.

The foreign exchanges no longer respond to the sentimental effects of the armistice. Business is flat all round, and operators seem unusually cautious; rates have simply fallen away through sheer inanition. In Amsterdam dealers offer 11 fl 41c but call for 11 fl 46c to the pound; the Paris cheque hovers around 26 francs to £1. Swiss and Scandinavian exchanges were unfavourable to London in the early part of the week, and sterling has not received much support in Spain. ■
